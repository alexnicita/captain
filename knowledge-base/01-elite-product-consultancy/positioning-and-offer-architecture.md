# Elite Product Consultancy: Positioning & Offer Architecture

## When to use this

Use when refining your consultancy from "general product help" to a premium, defensible offer.

## Operating model

1. **Narrow to a high-value wedge**
   - Pick one buyer + one painful business problem + one context (stage/industry).
   - Example: "Series A B2B SaaS teams needing activation + retention uplift within 90 days."
2. **Define outcome-led offers**
   - Productize around measurable business outcomes (not role-based staffing).
   - Package: diagnostic → intervention sprint → embedded enablement.
3. **Design proof loops**
   - Every engagement must produce a public-safe artifact: case metrics, anonymized teardown, framework.
4. **Price by value band**
   - Anchor fee to expected impact range and urgency.
5. **Enforce client-fit filter**
   - Reject low-fit opportunities early (weak sponsor, no data access, no execution owner).

## Offer stack (recommended)

- **Tier 1: Strategic Diagnostic (2-3 weeks)**
  - Goal: identify highest-leverage growth/retention bottleneck
  - Deliverable: prioritized opportunity map + 90-day plan
- **Tier 2: Outcome Sprint (6-10 weeks)**
  - Goal: ship changes and move a target metric
  - Deliverable: implemented experiments + impact readout
- **Tier 3: Capability Transfer (ongoing 1-3 months)**
  - Goal: make client team self-sufficient
  - Deliverable: operating cadence, playbooks, dashboards

## Qualification checklist

- Executive sponsor with authority and weekly availability
- Clear north-star and baseline metrics
- Access to product analytics + customer research signals
- Named internal owner for implementation
- Budget aligned with problem value (not hourly shopping)

## KPIs & thresholds

- Lead-to-qualified rate: **>25%** (if lower, positioning likely too broad)
- Win rate on qualified opportunities: **>35%**
- Gross margin: **>55%** (service business baseline discipline)
- Referenceable engagement rate: **>60%**
- Repeat revenue share: **>40%**

## Failure modes

- Selling talent augmentation as strategy work
- Over-customization that destroys delivery leverage
- No explicit business metric ownership
- Accepting misfit clients to fill pipeline gaps
