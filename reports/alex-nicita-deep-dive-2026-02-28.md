## 6) Risks

1. **Evidence risk:** positioning outruns publicly visible outcomes.
2. **Demand qualification risk:** broad service menu can attract mismatched inbound.
3. **Single-brand concentration risk:** heavy dependence on founder persona and direct founder time.

5. **Execution diffusion risk:** writing + investing + client delivery can fragment attention without operating cadence.

## 7) 30/60/90-Day Operating Plan for Seaport & Co.

## Days 0–30: Clarify, package, instrument
**Objectives**
- Sharpen service narrative and ICP.
- Establish pipeline instrumentation.

**Actions**
1. Define 2 primary ICPs (e.g., venture-backed seed/Series A; established SMB digital transformation).
2. Launch service pages for each core offer with scope, timeline, deliverables, and ideal fit.
3. Publish first two proof assets (even anonymized with concrete metrics).
4. Add lead routing + UTM discipline + CRM stage definitions.
5. Create founder content cadence (1 long-form + 2 short derivatives weekly).

**KPIs**
- 2 service pages live.
- 2 proof assets live.
- 100% inbound logged by source and intent.

## Days 31–60: Build credibility flywheel
**Objectives**
- Convert thought leadership into qualified conversations.
- Raise win-rate via clearer qualification.

**Actions**
1. Publish one “Operating Thesis” page linking writing themes to Seaport engagements.
2. Release 2 additional case assets (or technical teardown memos if NDAs block details).
3. Build partner/referral kit (deck + one-pager + intro copy).
4. Implement qualification rubric (urgency, budget, team readiness, strategic fit).
5. Start monthly “state of product infrastructure” brief for founder audience.

**KPIs**
- ≥4 total proof artifacts.
- ≥30% inbound conversion from first call to scoped proposal.
- ≥3 active referral partners.

## Days 61–90: Scale selectively
**Objectives**
- Improve throughput without quality degradation.
- De-risk founder bottleneck.

**Actions**
1. Productize at least one repeatable offer (fixed scope/price range).
2. Build lightweight delivery OS (templates, QA gates, weekly client scorecards).
3. Introduce bench/partner network for overflow specialization.
4. Establish quarterly account planning for top clients.
5. Run retrospective on channel ROI and prune low-yield activity.

**KPIs**
- One productized offer with repeatable margin profile.
- On-time delivery >90%.
- Founder time on delivery reduced by documented delegation plan.
