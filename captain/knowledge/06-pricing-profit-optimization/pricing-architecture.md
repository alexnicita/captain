# Pricing Architecture for Services and Productized Offers

## When to use this

Use when setting or revising pricing to improve profitability without degrading fit.

## Pricing architecture options

- **Time & materials** — use for uncertain scope; requires strict change control.
- **Fixed scope / fixed fee** — use for standardized, well-bounded work.
- **Outcome/value-based** — use when impact attribution is credible.
- **Hybrid** — base fee + performance-linked component.

## Operating model

1. **Segment willingness-to-pay by client archetype**
2. **Tie price metric to customer value driver** (e.g., revenue gain, cost reduction, risk reduction)
3. **Define packaging tiers** (good/better/best)
4. **Set guardrails**
   - Minimum fee floors
   - Discount authority limits
   - Scope-change pricing rules
5. **Run controlled pricing experiments**
   - Test one variable per cycle (anchor, tier names, guarantees, payment terms)

## Checklist

- Price is presented after value framing, not before
- Commercial proposals include assumptions and exclusions
- Discounts require explicit trade-offs (scope/time/case rights)
- Payment schedule aligns with risk and cashflow needs
- Post-deal review captures margin variance causes

## KPIs

- Average selling price (ASP)
- Gross margin by offer type
- Realized discount rate
- Proposal win rate by price band
- Time-to-cash / DSO trend

## Failure modes

- Uniform pricing across very different value contexts
- Habitual discounting to close deals
- Weak scope controls causing margin leakage
- No historical pricing data for decisions
