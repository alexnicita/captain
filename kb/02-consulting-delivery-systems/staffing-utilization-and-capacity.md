# Staffing, Utilization, and Capacity Management

## When to use this

Use for hiring pace decisions, bench control, and preventing delivery burnout.

## Operating model

1. **Forecast demand by skill cluster** (4, 8, 12-week views)
2. **Map capacity**
   - Committed, at-risk, and bench availability per person.
3. **Apply staffing rules**
   - No key role at >90% for more than 2 consecutive weeks.
   - Maintain minimal bench buffer (5-15%) for volatility.
4. **Escalate early**
   - Trigger contractor/partner support when forecasted overload >2 weeks.
5. **Post-engagement reset**
   - Debrief and redeploy staff with learning goals.

## Staffing checklist

- Roles matched to engagement complexity
- Explicit delivery lead per account
- No single point of failure on critical workstreams
- Handoff docs prepared before transitions
- Protected non-billable time for capability building

## KPIs

- Utilization by role (target bands by level)
- Bench rate and bench aging
- Overtime frequency (>45-50h/week flags)
- Gross margin variance by staffing plan vs actual
- Voluntary attrition and engagement pulse

## Failure modes

- Hiring reactively after overload already hits quality
- Maximizing short-term utilization at cost of burnout
- Overusing senior talent on work that should be templated/delegated
- No capacity forecast beyond 2 weeks
