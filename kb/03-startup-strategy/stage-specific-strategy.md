# Startup Strategy by Stage

## When to use this

Use to avoid applying late-stage playbooks too early (or vice versa).

## Stage priorities

### 0 -> 1 (Problem/Solution fit)

- Objective: prove painful problem and viable solution behavior.
- Focus: customer interviews, rapid prototypes, tight learning cycles.
- KPI emphasis: activation signal quality, interview-to-insight velocity.

### 1 -> 10 (Early traction)

- Objective: repeatable acquisition + activation + retention for a clear ICP.
- Focus: channel testing, onboarding conversion, core loop reliability.
- KPI emphasis: retention cohorts, payback period signals, expansion behavior.

### 10 -> 100 (Scale)

- Objective: strengthen growth efficiency and organizational execution.
- Focus: process hardening, management layer, capital efficiency.
- KPI emphasis: CAC payback, gross margin consistency, net revenue retention.

## Strategic sequence checklist

- Clear "where to play" statement (segment, use case, wedge)
- Explicit "why now" tied to market shift or enabling technology
- Bottleneck diagnosis updated monthly
- Top 3 strategic bets with owner, budget, and kill criteria
- Quarterly narrative refresh for team and investors

## KPI set (cross-stage)

- Time-to-value for new users
- Retention (logo/user/revenue as relevant)
- Contribution margin trend
- Experiment velocity and hit rate
- Runway months under base and stress scenarios

## Failure modes

- Pursuing channel scale before retention quality
- Chasing enterprise deals without product readiness
- Measuring activity (meetings/features) instead of behavior/outcomes
- Strategy drift from founder attention fragmentation
